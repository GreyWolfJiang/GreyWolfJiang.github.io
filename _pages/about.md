<div class='paper-box-text' markdown="1">

**Effect of Exogenous Glycine on Yield and Quality of Grafted Watermelon with Different Rootstocks**

**Jiang Cheng**

- Investigated whether exogenous glycine can substitute nitrate as a nitrogen source for watermelon growth and development, combining grafting technology to study the effects on different rootstocks.
- Results: Grafted seedlings (*Cucurbita moschata* cv. Jinxin No.2) supplemented with exogenous glycine exhibited significantly increased fruit yield, higher free amino acid content, elevated soluble solids (Brix), and markedly reduced nitrate content compared to self-grafted (*Zaojia 8424*) seedlings.
- Data processing: Growth indicators and quality parameters measured and analyzed using **Excel**, **SPSS**, and visualized with **Origin**.
